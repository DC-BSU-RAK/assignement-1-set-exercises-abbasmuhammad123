Overview

Stranger Things is a science fiction horror drama television series created by the Duffer Brothers for Netflix. Set in the fictional town of Hawkins, Indiana, in the 1980s, the series combines supernatural elements with nostalgic references to 1980s pop culture.

Visit Official Site

Plot

The story begins in November 1983, when Will Byers is abducted by a creature from the Upside Down, an alternate dimension discovered by a government laboratory through experiments on a young girl named Eleven who has psychokinetic abilities. Will's friends, family, and local police chief Jim Hopper search for him, uncovering government conspiracies and supernatural mysteries along the way.

As the series progresses through multiple seasons, the friends and their families continue to battle the Upside Down's threats to their town and the world. The story explores the bonds of friendship, adolescence, and confronting fear in a small town plagued by supernatural occurrences.

Main Characters

Eleven (El)

A young girl with psychokinetic abilities who escaped from Hawkins Laboratory. She was subject to experiments that opened the gate to the Upside Down. Eleven forms a strong bond with Mike and his friends as they help her evade government agents.

Jim Hopper

Hawkins' police chief who investigates Will's disappearance. A troubled man dealing with personal trauma, he becomes a father figure to Eleven and a crucial ally in the fight against the Upside Down threats.

Mike Wheeler

The de facto leader of the friend group who develops a special connection with Eleven. He's loyal, determined, and often takes the lead in the adventures to save Will and later battle the Mind Flayer.

Joyce Byers

Will's mother who refuses to believe her son is dead. Her determination leads her to discover the supernatural truth behind his disappearance, often appearing frantic but proving remarkably perceptive.
